## 1. Checklist Usage Rules

Use this checklist only after:

- A redacted borrower profile has been validated.
- Verified product criteria are available.
- Hard gates and weighted scoring are complete.
- A qualified human selects a route for further review.
- The criteria-verification date is visible.
- Borrower authorization is documented in the approved system.

This checklist prepares a file. It does not approve the borrower, confirm
eligibility or affordability, authorize automatic submission, sign or accept an
agreement, or replace current provider instructions.

## 3. Borrower Authorization

- [ ] Analysis authorization is confirmed.
- [ ] Intended provider or destination is identified.
- [ ] Borrower consent for the intended submission is documented.

## 4. Identity and Ownership Verification

- [ ] Identity-verification status is recorded.
- [ ] Beneficial ownership is recorded.
- [ ] Ownership percentages are consistent.

- [ ] Any guarantor requirement has been reverified.
- [ ] Restricted identity documents remain in approved storage.
- [ ] The checklist uses secure references rather than restricted identifiers.

## 5. Business Verification

- [ ] Legal business name and DBA are verified.
- [ ] Entity type is verified.
- [ ] Formation and operating states are recorded.
- [ ] Business start date is verified.
- [ ] Time in business is recalculated in complete months.
- [ ] Industry and NAICS classification are reviewed.
- [ ] State and industry restrictions are rechecked.
- [ ] Material business-record conflicts are resolved.

## 6. Revenue Documentation

- [ ] Average monthly revenue uses a stated review period.
- [ ] Annual revenue period is stated.
- [ ] Gross revenue is not confused with net income.
- [ ] Borrower-reported figures are labeled.
- [ ] Verified figures are supported.
- [ ] Partial months are not treated as complete without disclosure.
- [ ] Seasonal patterns are documented.
- [ ] Revenue inconsistencies are resolved or escalated.

## 7. Bank Documentation

- [ ] Approved bank-link status is recorded.
- [ ] Primary operating account is identified.
- [ ] Review period is stated.
- [ ] Average balance is calculated consistently.
- [ ] Negative days and returned items use a defined period.
- [ ] Deposit timing is reviewed against proposed payment timing.
- [ ] Account and routing numbers are not copied into the brief.
- [ ] Raw statements remain in approved secure storage.

## 8. Existing-Obligation Verification

- [ ] Active business obligations are listed.
- [ ] Monthly payment totals are current.
- [ ] Daily, weekly, and monthly frequencies are distinguished.
- [ ] Existing funding-position count is verified.
- [ ] Payoff-at-closing assumptions are documented.
- [ ] Debt schedule agrees with available records.
- [ ] Conflicting balances are resolved.
- [ ] Stacking or refinancing risks are reviewed.

## 9. Use-of-Funds Documentation

- [ ] Product duration is compared with the useful life of the expense.
- [ ] Any short-term/long-term mismatch is explicitly reviewed.

## 23. Product-Specific Checklist Variants

### A. Business Line of Credit

- [ ] Recurring use is documented.
- [ ] Draw and repayment mechanics are understood.
- [ ] Minimum draw, maintenance, variable pricing, and renewal rules are confirmed.
- [ ] A fixed-term alternative was compared.

### B. Term Loan

- [ ] Lump-sum need is defined.
- [ ] Term matches useful life.
- [ ] Payment schedule, amortization, balloon, and prepayment rules are reviewed.
- [ ] Cash-flow support is documented.

### C. Equipment Financing

- [ ] Current quote or invoice is available.
- [ ] Vendor and equipment details are verified.
- [ ] Useful life and term are aligned.
- [ ] Down payment, collateral, delivery, and insurance rules are confirmed.

### D. Invoice Financing or Factoring

- [ ] Aging report and invoices are current.
- [ ] Customer concentration is reviewed.
- [ ] Recourse and collections mechanics are confirmed.
- [ ] Customer notice, disputes, offsets, and chargebacks are reviewed.

### E. Short-Term Working Capital

- [ ] Temporary gap and resolving event are defined.
- [ ] Frequent-payment pressure is reviewed.
- [ ] Existing positions are verified.
- [ ] Long-term alternatives and stacking risk are discussed.

### F. SBA-Related or Bank Financing

- [ ] Current program and lender requirements are verified.
- [ ] Tax returns and financial statements are complete.
- [ ] Ownership and personal financial records remain in approved storage.
- [ ] Collateral, guarantee, timeline, and plan requirements are confirmed.

### G. Inventory Financing

- [ ] Supplier, inventory, sell-through, and margin are documented.
- [ ] Obsolescence and seasonality are reviewed.
- [ ] Repayment source and downside scenario are documented.

### H. Commercial Real Estate Financing

- [ ] Property, purpose, ownership, and equity are verified.
- [ ] Contract, rent roll, leases, appraisal, title, environmental, and insurance needs are reviewed.
- [ ] Cash flow and closing timeline are reviewed.

## 24. Do-Not-Submit Conditions

Do not proceed when:

- Authorization is absent or withdrawn.
- Criteria are Stale, Unverified, or conflicting.
- A hard gate failed without an authorized exception.
- Required documents are materially incomplete.
- Identity, ownership, revenue, or obligations materially conflict.
- The route depends on invented criteria.
- The user asks to conceal negative information.
- An override lacks evidence and signoff.
- Product use does not fit.
- Payment pressure has not been reviewed.
- Restricted data would travel through an unapproved channel.
- Final human review is incomplete.
